# Brainstorming Session Results

**Facilitator:** LaszloHorvath
**Date:** 2026-06-11 (continued 2026-06-12)

## Session Overview

**Topic:** Commuter car-sharing mobile app for regular short-distance urban commuters
**Goals:** Explore features, business model, UX, engagement mechanics, and differentiation strategies for a daily commuter rideshare app targeting city suburb-to-center traffic reduction

### Session Setup

User's core concept: A mobile app connecting regular daily commuters (same origin/destination, same time windows) for shared rides — focused on the suburban-to-city-center morning/evening rush pattern. Key differentiator from existing solutions: recurring, habitual use vs. one-off trips; short urban distances vs. long intercity journeys.

## Technique Selection

**Approach:** Progressive Technique Flow
**Journey Design:** Systematic development from exploration to action

**Progressive Techniques:**

- **Phase 1 - Exploration:** What If Scenarios — challenge all assumptions, generate maximum breadth
- **Phase 2 - Pattern Recognition:** Six Thinking Hats — analyze ideas through 6 distinct perspectives
- **Phase 3 - Development:** SCAMPER Method — systematically refine top concepts through 7 lenses
- **Phase 4 - Action Planning:** Constraint Mapping — identify real vs. imagined blockers and pathways forward

## Phase 2: Pattern Recognition — Six Thinking Hats

*Status: IN PROGRESS — White Hat underway*

**Focus Ideas Selected for Stress-Testing:**

1. Corridor-based route matching with Maps integration
2. Two-sided trust & verification system
3. Recurring vs. ad-hoc ride modes
4. Revenue model: distance-scaled fares + 5% platform cut + in-app wallet
5. Cold-start / launch strategy: Budapest business districts + curated first matches + early adopter incentives

### ⚪ White Hat — Facts & Data

*What do we actually know vs. what are we assuming?*

**Assumption gaps identified:**

| Idea | Known | Assumption to Validate |
|------|-------|------------------------|
| Route matching | Corridor proximity is the right model | Acceptable detour tolerance for Budapest commuters (5 min? 10 min?) — unknown |
| Trust & verification | Verification reduces risk | Whether Hungarian commuters will actually trust strangers enough — no data yet |
| Recurring vs. ad-hoc | Both modes are needed | Hybrid work may make ad-hoc the majority use case, not the fallback |
| Revenue model | 5% of distance-scaled fares is the model | Whether average fare × rides/month × users covers operating costs — not yet modelled |
| Cold-start | Budapest business districts have commuter density | Minimum viable density (rides per corridor per day) for matching to feel useful — unknown |

*White Hat complete.*

**Key finding:** Trust/verification and cold-start density are the same problem — you can't solve one without the other.

| Idea | Clarified Position |
|------|-------------------|
| Route matching | Detour tolerance: percentage-based, 10–20% on both time AND distance; driver-configurable per route |
| Trust & verification | Most critical unknown; blue check model preferred over hard-gated tiers |
| Recurring vs. ad-hoc | Both modes equally valid; which dominates is irrelevant — coverage matters |
| Revenue | Must cover operating costs minimum; mission over margin; modelling needed |
| Cold-start | #1 existential risk; linked directly to trust problem; chicken-and-egg |

### 🔴 Red Hat — Emotions & Gut Instinct

*What feels right or wrong, even without logical justification.*

| Idea | Emotional Response |
|------|-------------------|
| Route matching | Intuitive — "they pass near me, I join" is a natural mental model |
| Trust & verification | Reframed: phone + email mandatory; driver's licence for drivers; everything else = optional blue check (aspirational, not gated) |
| Ride posting UX | Must be dead simple — workday/occasional toggle + day/time picker |
| Payment | Card-first at launch; in-app wallet as later feature once trust is established |
| First ride emotional target | Connection, relaxation, social good, savings. Failure state: scared, frustrated, uncomfortable |

**Key finding:** Blue check framing is a significant UX upgrade — users start immediately, verification effort is visibly rewarded, no interrogation feeling.

---

### ⚫ Black Hat — Risks & Caution

*What could go wrong or be exploited.*

| Idea | Risk & Resolution |
|------|------------------|
| Route matching | Punctuality mismatch: self-correcting via ratings + blocking with reason |
| Trust & verification | False security risk: platform must communicate "verified = identity confirmed, not character guaranteed" |
| Recurring obligation trap | Eliminated by design: passengers book single rides; drivers post recurring offers; withdrawal allowed with late-cancellation penalty |
| Driver commercialisation | Fare caps and positioning must prevent taxi-alternative behaviour; cost-sharing not side income |
| Cold-start imbalance | #1 existential risk — not just density but driver/passenger balance; losing either side kills the network |

**Key finding:** Single-ride booking model solves multiple problems simultaneously — no obligation trap, natural churn prevention, clean cancellation logic.

---

### 🟡 Yellow Hat — Benefits & Optimism

*Best realistic outcomes and underrated upsides.*

| Idea | Upside |
|------|--------|
| Route matching | Zero behaviour change for drivers — they just get a passenger who fits their existing route |
| Trust & verification | Blue check creates virtuous loop — verification effort rewarded with better matches over time |
| Recurring booking | Tension identified: regular commuters shouldn't need to re-book daily — trusted pair fast-track needed |
| Mission & non-greed | Non-greed as competitive moat — users feel like participants, not customers; hard to replicate |
| Word-of-mouth | Happy users = primary marketing channel; product quality IS the marketing strategy |

**Key finding:** Tension between single-ride booking (Black Hat) and daily friction for regulars (Yellow Hat) — resolved by trusted pair fast-track: once established, booking becomes one tap.

---

### 🟢 Green Hat — Creativity & New Angles

*Unexpected ideas and lateral connections.*

| Idea | New Angle |
|------|-----------|
| Route matching | Map visualization: show nearby drivers/passengers with departure times; smart filtering at scale (best 2-3 of 20+ matches shown) |
| Curated first match | Good in principle; logistically hard early on — needs alternative cold-start solution |
| Standing ride | Auto-confirm for trusted pairs; off-platform coordination risk accepted — mission accomplished either way |
| Cost transparency | Public operating cost display reinforces non-greed positioning |
| Launch strategy | "First ride day" event considered; expensive but high publicity ROI |

**Key finding:** Map visualization solves the cold-start social proof problem — showing active corridors before signup converts anxiety into confidence.

---

### 🔵 Blue Hat — Process & Control

*Right order of things; sequencing and priorities.*

| Idea | Sequencing Decision |
|------|-------------------|
| Map visualization | Route registration at launch; map appears as soon as first users register — don't delay |
| Blue check extras | "Finish your profile" nudge post-onboarding; unlocked progressively by user growth milestone |
| Both ride modes | Recurring AND ad-hoc both available at launch — too much friction to split |
| In-app wallet | Unlocked after 10 successful rides (either role) — earned access; reduces fraud risk |
| Launch / growth strategy | No single launch event. Milestone celebrations instead: 100th / 1,000th ride as public moments; gift first 100 users to complete 10 rides; prize draw from first 100 / 500 / 1,000 registered users |

**Key finding:** Milestone celebration model creates multiple marketing moments over time, rewards behaviour not just registration, and scales naturally with growth. The 10-ride wallet unlock ties financial trust to earned platform experience.

## Phase 2 Complete — Six Thinking Hats Summary

**Stress-tested ideas:** Corridor route matching, two-sided trust & verification, recurring vs. ad-hoc modes, revenue model, cold-start strategy

**Most critical insight across all hats:** Trust + cold-start are the same problem. Solve the first 500 users' experience with curated matches, map visualization, and milestone incentives — and the rest follows from word-of-mouth.

**Strongest new ideas generated in Phase 2:**
- Map visualization as social proof before sign-up
- Trusted pair fast-track for recurring commuters (one-tap booking)
- Milestone celebration model replacing launch event (100th ride, 1,000th ride, first 100 users to complete 10 rides, prize draws)
- In-app wallet unlocked at 10 rides earned
- Cost transparency display
